LINUS TROVALDS

Description

Linus Benedict Torvalds is the world's most famous computer programmer. Linus Torvalds created the Linux kernel and oversees open source development of the widely-used Linux operating system of our time.

  • At age 10 Torvalds began to dabble in computer programming on his grandfather’s Commodore VIC-20.
  • In 1991 he posted a message on the Internet to alert other PC users to his new system i.e linux
  • On August 25, 1991, he announced his initial creation on the MINIX newsgroup comp.os.minix
  • In 1994 Linux kernel (original code) version 1.0 was released.
  • Linux became popular in the late 1990s when competitors of Microsoft began taking the upstart OS seriously.
  • Linus Torvalds invented Git 15 years ago in order to continue development of the Linux kernel.
  • In 1990 he was awarded a MacArthur fellowship, the so-called “genius award” that gives recipients a substantial financial stipend with no strings attached.
  • In 1998 Linus was honoured with Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF) Pioneer Award.
  • In 1999 he received ‘one of the top 100 innovators, under the age of 35’ in the world’ by MIT Technology Review TR100, in 2000
  • he received Lovelace Medal, by British Computer Society, in 2012 he was the recipient of the Millennium Technology Prize.
In open-source, we feel strongly that to really do something well, you have to get a lot of people involved. – Linus Torvalds.
